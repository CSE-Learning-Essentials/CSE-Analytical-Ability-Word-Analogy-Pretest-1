SECTION 2: ANALYTICAL ABILITY

PART 1: WORD MEANING

10-ITEM TEST | PASSING SCORE: 8

Directions: Each question below consists of a related pair of words. From among the suggested answers, choose the word or group of words that best expresses a similar relationship from the original pair.

1. OBSCURE : VAGUE ::

2. BENEVOLENT : MALEVOLENT ::

3. IGNORANCE : MISUNDERSTANDING ::

4. JUDGE : COURTROOM ::

5. PETAL : FLOWER ::

6. SCISSORS : CUT ::

7. QUARRY : MARBLE ::

8. RAIN : FLOOD ::

9. FREEZING : COLD ::

10. HUNGRY : STARVING ::

Answer Explanations:

  1. C. ancient : old
    Relationship: Synonyms.
    OBSCURE : VAGUE :: ancient : old
    Explanation: Obscure means “unclear, not easily understood,” and vague means “indefinite, uncertain.” In the same way, ancient and old are synonyms that both describe great age.
  2. C. honest : deceitful
    Relationship: Antonyms.
    BENEVOLENT : MALEVOLENT :: honest : deceitful
    Explanation: Benevolent means “kind, well-meaning,” while malevolent means “evil, ill-willed.” Similarly, honest is the opposite of deceitful, making both pairs antonyms.
  3. D. drought : famine
    Relationship: Cause to Effect.
    IGNORANCE : MISUNDERSTANDING :: drought : famine
    Explanation: Ignorance (lack of knowledge) often causes misunderstanding. Likewise, drought (lack of rain) can lead to famine (lack of food). Both pairs show a cause-and-effect relationship.
  4. C. professor : classroom
    Relationship: Person to Workplace.
    JUDGE : COURTROOM :: professor : classroom
    Explanation: A judge works in a courtroom. A professor works in a classroom. Both pairs show a person and the place where their job is carried out.
  5. E. wheel : car
    Relationship: Part to Whole.
    PETAL : FLOWER :: wheel : car
    Explanation: A petal is part of a flower. A wheel is part of a car. Both pairs illustrate a part-to-whole relationship.
  6. C. thermometer : measure
    Relationship: Object to Function.
    SCISSORS : CUT :: thermometer : measure
    Explanation: The primary function of scissors is to cut. The primary function of a thermometer is to measure temperature. Both pairs show an object and its function.
  7. A. mine : coal
    Relationship: Source to Product.
    QUARRY : MARBLE :: mine : coal
    Explanation: A quarry is the source of marble. A mine is the source of coal. Both pairs show a place and the material obtained from it.
  8. C. fire : smoke
    Relationship: Cause to Effect.
    RAIN : FLOOD :: fire : smoke
    Explanation: Heavy rain can cause flooding. Fire produces smoke. Both pairs demonstrate a natural cause leading to its effect.
  9. B. scorching : hot
    Relationship: Degree of Intensity.
    FREEZING : COLD :: scorching : hot
    Explanation: Freezing is an extreme degree of cold. Scorching is an extreme degree of hot. Both pairs show intensity of the same quality.
  10. B. tired : exhausted
    Relationship: Degree of Intensity.
    HUNGRY : STARVING :: tired : exhausted
    Explanation: Starving is an extreme degree of hungry. Exhausted is an extreme degree of tired. Both pairs represent a base condition and its more intense form.
